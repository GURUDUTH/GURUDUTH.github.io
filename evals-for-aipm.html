By Guruduth Rao

EVALS FOR PM/AIPM's

Let's keep the introduction simple and start with an example.

Consider you or your team have built an AI application, your team might have used various frameworks, one particular LLM or a combination of LLM's and tools to do so in order to build it, but how do you know what you have built actually works or if the application satisfies your desired outcome? You as a PM/AIPM can understand the outcomes/metrics/evaluations of these applications using something commonly called as EVALS.

But before diving into what EVALS are and how do we use it, let's actually understand something that will help you figure out what type of evals you will be dealing with. As a PM you have to know about two types of EVALS:

  • You can evaluate a large language model
  • You can evaluate an AI application built on top of this LLM

As a PM you will mainly be dealing with applications that are built on top of these large language models and how you evaluate those applications.

Why Application Evaluations Matter

Now here's something to think about: since we are anyhow using an LLM to build an AI application, wouldn't an LLM evaluation be sufficient before using it to build something? Why conduct an AI application evaluation separately? And if an LLM already has a good benchmark, why conduct an evaluation at all?

The answer to this is quite simple. Yes, often an LLM with better benchmarks would be sufficient but only as long as it's a simple application. But as an AIPM, simple applications are not something you deal with very commonly, because you won't be building applications to solve simple or similar tasks.

The solutions/features you and your team would have built may be solving a complex problem that includes factors such as:

  • RAG (retrieval augmented generation)
  • Prompt templates
  • Model fine-tuning
  • Agentic frameworks
  • Quantisation

All these can affect the output quality of an AI application built on top of an LLM. In simple words, if you were to ask a question to an LLM and the same question to your AI application, the answer would differ, because your application would have been built to solve a specific problem instead of being a general LLM for chat or Q/A.
